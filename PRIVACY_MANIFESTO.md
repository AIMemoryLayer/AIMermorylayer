# 🛡️ PRIVACY_MANIFESTO.md

**Data Sovereignty for AI Memory.**

In the age of AI, memory is more than just data—it is a digital extension of an individual or organization's knowledge and identity. AIMemoryLayer is committed to the following principles:

---

## 🤝 1. Data Sovereignty

Users always own their data. AIMemoryLayer provides the tools to store and retrieve memory, but we do not lay claim to the underlying knowledge. Users should be able to export or delete their entire memory layer at any time.

## 🔒 2. Encryption by Default

All memory records (embeddings and metadata) must be encrypted at rest and in transit. We prioritize Zero-Knowledge architectures wherever possible, ensuring that even the platform operators cannot peek into sensitive memory without authorization.

## 🏗️ 3. Granular Access Control

Knowledge is power, and power should be controlled. Our RBAC (Role-Based Access Control) system ensures that agents only "remember" what they are explicitly authorized to know. No cross-contamination between user memories.

## 🕵️ 4. Auditability & Transparency

Every memory access, retrieval, and injection should be logged and auditable. Users must have full visibility into what their agents are remembering and why.

## ♻️ 5. The "Right to Forget"

AI agents shouldn't be trapped in a permanent state of the past. AIMemoryLayer supports TTL (Time-To-Live) for memories and explicit "forget" commands to purge old or inaccurate data.

---

_This manifesto is a living document and will evolve as we refine our privacy-preserving technologies._
